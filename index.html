A Life of Compassion and Service

Mother Teresa was an inspiration for people around the world for her selfless service to the poor and destitute.

Early Life

Rugadh Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu (Máthair Teresa) ar 26 Lúnasa 1910 i Skopje, a bhí mar chuid den Impireacht Ottoman (an Mhacadóin Thuaidh anois). Tá a céad ainm, Anjezë, cosúil le Agnes, agus ciallaíonn "Gonxhe" "bud bláthanna" san Albáinis. Baisteadh í an lá tar éis a breithe agus breithníodh níos déanaí ar an lá sin, 27 Lúnasa, a breithlá fíor.​
Ba í an leanbh ab óige í ag Nikollë agus Dranafile Bojaxhiu. Fuair ​​a hathair, a raibh baint aige le polaitíocht Albanach, bás nuair a bhí sí ocht mbliana d’aois. Ba as Prizren na Cosaive é, le fréamhacha teaghlaigh san Albáin, agus is dócha gur as sráidbhaile in aice le Gjakova a mháthair.​

At 18, Anjezë left home in 1928 to join the Sisters of Loreto in Ireland, where she learned English to become a missionary. She never saw her family again after leaving. In 1929, she arrived in India and began her novitiate in Darjeeling, learning Bengali and teaching at St. Teresa's School. She took her first vows in 1931 and chose the name Teresa, after Thérèse de Lisieux.​
By 1937, she took her solemn vows and became known as Mother Teresa while serving as a teacher at a convent school in Calcutta. In 1944, she became headmistress, but the surrounding poverty, especially during the 1943 Bengal famine and 1946 religious violence, deeply affected her.​ ​
